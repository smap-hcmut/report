# Acknowledgements

The completion of this thesis would not have been possible without the support of many individuals and technical resources surrounding the SMAP project.

First, sincere appreciation is extended to the academic supervisor for the methodological guidance, the emphasis on technical rigor, and the insistence that system claims must be supported by verifiable evidence. This perspective strongly shaped the way the thesis was organized, especially in maintaining consistency between requirements, design, implementation, and evaluation.

Appreciation is also extended to the maintainers and contributors of the SMAP codebase. Their work in building service documentation, route definitions, migration files, runtime manifests, and testing assets made it possible to analyze the platform at a meaningful depth. Even when some artifacts were incomplete or distributed across different services, the existing codebase still provided a valuable foundation for reconstructing the architectural and implementation logic of the system.

Finally, gratitude is expressed to the broader engineering and research communities whose tools, frameworks, and architectural references contributed indirectly to this work. Open-source ecosystems such as Go, Python, Gin, FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Redis, Kafka, RabbitMQ, Qdrant, Docker, and Kubernetes not only enabled the implementation of the system itself, but also shaped the technical vocabulary and architectural understanding used throughout the thesis.
